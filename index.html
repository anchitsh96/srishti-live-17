Story of Space explores the concept of Home in their projects displayed at the metro station

Most of us think of home as the house or city we were born or brought up in. Where we spent most of us childhood days and made most number of memories.

Erased Wings

We have chosen to take the endangered birds and animals of Karnataka and also the tribes of Karnataka and their art which shall be further incorporated in our totem pole. We are the reason these birds are coming to the point of extinction.

Home as object

We tend to identify with objects of sentiment or belonging, where the familiarity reminds us of home. There is an emotional connection associated with these objects, which can be traced back to the memories associated with it, or the role it plays in the lives of people. The project will attempt to harness this association between people and such objects.

Home as digital space

This project aims to show the growing attachment to an intangible, virtual home with advancing technology. To be at home is to know where you are; it means to inhabit a secure centre and to be oriented in a space, conventionally a physical space.

Morphing House

A home as a space is a representation of us. The architecture of our home is used to reflect our culture but now with modern times and cultural globalisation we all are homogenised and all chase the same physical modern aesthetics of home.

Srishti Live 2017

The Interim Semester at Srishti Institute of Art, Design and Technology is an annual initiative that aims to nurture emerging creativity by engaging with contemporary artists and artistic practices. It culminates in a showcase of projects produced and presented during the four weeks of the Interim.

Through the duration of the Interim semester, students work with National and International contemporary artists who are invited through the Srisht interim Artist-in-Residency Program (SAIR). The artists either bring in their on-going projects, or create and facilitate projects – all of which explore socially engaging, participatory and context-led content.

Srishti provides this opportunity for students to go beyond the classroom and enter the public realm, where they delve into creative processes of research, re-enactment, documentation, facilitation and production. The range of projects that take place explore culturally relevant subject matter that is often contentious. They comprise of community based efforts, interactions between art and the sciences, and artistic and curatorial interventions that are site-specific.
